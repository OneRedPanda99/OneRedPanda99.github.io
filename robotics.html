The Robot

Designed to clean
a beach.

Our robot was designed to locate, pick up, and deliver trash to trash bins — specifically on the beach. The competition scenario was environmental cleanup, and we built something that could actually handle that task autonomously.

The design had to solve a real problem: find trash, grab it, move it somewhere. That meant working through sensors, grippers, and navigation. A lot of iteration before we got to something that worked under competition pressure.

Mission Objective
Locate, pick up, and deliver trash to designated bins — simulating autonomous beach cleanup. Scored on accuracy, speed, and reliability across multiple runs.

My Role

Keeping everyone
on the same page.

My job was coordination. When you have a group of people all with different ideas about how to build something, someone has to say "okay, here's what we're doing." That was me.

01
Ran Every Meeting
Coordinated every team meeting, set the agenda, and kept things moving instead of everyone just talking past each other.
02
Unified the Direction
Made sure every team member agreed on what we were building and what the robot was doing. No conflicting builds, no wasted work.
03
Competed Under Pressure
Showed up on competition day and applied everything the team built under real conditions. 4th place — first placement in 6 years.

How the FTC Program Started

I was standing in line
to get my award.

I got the information for FTC while standing in line to receive my Beta Club award. A peer started talking to me and just laid out how their club worked, how FTC functioned, all of it. By the time I walked away with that award I already had a plan. I got back home and immediately started working on building the program at Cardinal Newman. That's how fast it happened. I'm the Team Captain now and we're looking forward to competing in the next event.
