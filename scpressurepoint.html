What it is

It's my idea.
On my terms.

I started doing pressure washing and odd jobs at 14. It's not a fully licensed business — this is here for whenever I decide to take it a step further. Right now I mainly use the site to show clients before and after pictures and make sure they know I'm unlicensed. Honest and upfront.

The site gives me something to point people to so they can see the work. It's a portfolio for the pressure washing the same way this site is a portfolio for the tech stuff.

How it works

Lead comes in.
Here's what happens.

01
Lead comes in
Someone reaches out — usually by word of mouth or through the site.
02
Aerial quote from Google Earth
I pull up the property on Google Earth, get a rough sense of the square footage and what needs doing, and give them a ballpark number. I make it clear nothing's final until I see it in person.
03
In-person assessment
Show up, look at the actual job, lock in the real price, and schedule it.
04
Do the job
Show up, do the work, document before and afters. High standard regardless of the size of the job.

A Job That Stands Out

I worked with a partner once.
Never again.

I did a job with a friend when we were partners under a different name. After the job he wanted more money than he worked for. That was bad enough on its own — but what made it worse was that after that job we stopped working together entirely, because I couldn't deal with that. I learned fast that when it comes to work and money, you find out who people really are pretty quickly. I've operated on my own since. I know what I did, I know what I earned, and I know what I owe myself.

Why build it yourself

Nothing holding
me back.

Why not just use Squarespace?

Cost
It was free to make it myself. No monthly subscription for a pressure washing side hustle.
Interest
I was already into web development. It was a chance to build something real and actually use what I was learning.
Control
I didn't have anything holding me back from making it exactly how I had it in my head. No limits from a template or a drag-and-drop editor.
