How it started

I saw a kid at school
using one. That was it.

I was interested in making my own stuff and saw a kid using a 3D printer at my school. That was enough. I wanted to do something like that, so I figured out how to make it happen.

It started with wanting to make things. Not buy them, not download a model someone else designed — actually make them. A 3D printer turns an idea in your head into something physical. That was exactly what I was looking for.

The Learning Curve

A print fell off the bed.
Turns out you have to actually clean it.

My first real failure was a print that fell off the bed mid-print. Bed wasn't clean, so the filament wouldn't stick. Once I learned how to properly prep and clean the print bed, the adhesion issues pretty much went away. It's one of those things where the fix is obvious once you know it, but you have to mess up first to get there. That's kind of the whole deal with 3D printing.
