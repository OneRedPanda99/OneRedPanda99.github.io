The Origin

It started with
a frustrating problem.

When I was 10 years old my favorite game to play with my friends was Minecraft. However, there was one problem that made it all so very frustrating to play. We played on an edition that allowed you to join your friend's world as long as they were in it. Well my friends weren't online as much as I was and we had different schedules so that made it extremely difficult to play.

So, I asked my IT Expert of a father how to set up a Minecraft server. Through him I was able to learn how to setup and create as many servers I wanted and slowly learned about plugins and then solved problems of cross platform and eventually was able to play with all of my friends on the same server.

The Growth

One server turned into
a community.

A month before I turned 11 I got the Java edition of Minecraft and that led to more server compatibility issues and learning curves, but also more capabilities. By the time I was 13 I had grown a community to 80+ members and 20+ daily active members on my server.

What went from wanting a place to play Minecraft with my friends turned into a larger but small scale Minecraft Community — something I could sit back and be proud of. If it weren't for the academic and emotional stresses of going into High School, it would be safe to say I could've continued to grow this community and learned even more.
